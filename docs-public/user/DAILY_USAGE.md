# DAILY_USAGE.md

## Purpose

This document explains how Super Mechanic is used in daily operations.

It is intended for:
- workshop staff
- managers
- administrative users

Goal:
Operate the system efficiently without needing technical knowledge.

---

## Daily Workflow Overview

A typical day using Super Mechanic consists of:

1. Checking workload
2. Reviewing priorities
3. Processing vehicles
4. Following up with clients
5. Managing appointments
6. Closing tasks and processes

---

## Step 1 — Start the Day

Go to:

→ Dashboard → "Mi trabajo"

Here you will see:

- 🔴 Critical items (urgent)
- 🟡 Attention items (important)
- ⚪ Normal items

### What to do:

1. Start with **Critical**
2. Then move to **Attention**
3. Finally, handle **Normal**

---

## Step 2 — Work on Tasks

Tasks come from:

- CRM follow-ups
- internal actions
- process-related work

Each task includes:

- title
- related client or vehicle
- due date
- status

### Actions:

- open task
- complete it
- add notes if needed

---

## Step 3 — Manage Vehicles in Process

Each vehicle goes through a **process**.

Examples:
- maintenance
- delivery preparation
- paperwork

### What to check:

- current step
- pending actions
- missing approvals

### Typical flow:

1. Receive vehicle
2. Diagnose issue
3. Perform work
4. Final review
5. Mark as ready

---

## Step 4 — Handle Alerts

Alerts are automatic system signals.

Examples:

- overdue tasks
- delayed processes
- follow-up needed

### Important:

Alerts are already calculated by the system.  
You only need to act on them.

---

## Step 5 — Manage Appointments

Appointments represent scheduled work.

### Actions:

- check today's appointments
- assign responsible staff
- update status after completion

---

## Step 6 — Follow Up with Clients

Use CRM to:

- track opportunities
- follow up leads
- maintain relationships

### Actions:

- check pending follow-ups
- contact clients
- update status

---

## Step 7 — Close Work

At the end of each activity:

- complete tasks
- update process steps
- confirm appointments
- finalize invoices if needed

---

## Example: Maintenance Workflow

1. Vehicle arrives
2. Create or open process
3. Add diagnostic notes
4. Detect additional issues
5. Wait for approval
6. Perform work
7. Final check
8. Mark as completed

---

## Example: Vehicle Delivery Workflow

1. Confirm payment
2. Process insurance
3. Process license plate
4. Final inspection
5. Deliver vehicle

---

## Priorities Explained

### 🔴 Critical
- overdue tasks
- delayed processes
- urgent alerts

👉 Must be handled immediately

---

### 🟡 Attention
- upcoming deadlines
- follow-ups
- pending approvals

👉 Handle during the day

---

### ⚪ Normal
- routine work
- non-urgent tasks

👉 Handle when time allows

---

## Best Practices

- Start every day with "Mi trabajo"
- Do not skip overdue items
- Keep processes updated
- Close tasks as soon as completed
- Use notes for clarity
- Avoid leaving items in unclear states

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Ignoring alerts
- Leaving tasks open after completion
- Not updating process steps
- Working outside the system
- Delaying follow-ups

---

## Key Principle

The system works best when:

👉 everything is updated in real time

If actions are not recorded, the system cannot help.

---

## Final Note

Super Mechanic is designed to:

- reduce manual tracking
- improve visibility
- organize daily work

Use it as your **single source of operational truth**.
